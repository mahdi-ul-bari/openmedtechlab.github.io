OpenXamp: Open source biosignal amplifier

OpenXamp is a cost-effective, open-source bio-signal amplifier designed to filter and amplify physiological signals for laboratory and DIY experiments. It features a high common-mode-rejection-ratio (CMRR) pre-amplifier for differential signal detection, with an option to short the inverting input for single-ended use. The device includes band-pass filtering and adjustable amplification to tailor the signal strength. Users can either utilize the amplified analog signal directly or convert it to digital via an integrated analog-to-digital converter (ADC) and microcontroller (MCU). The digitized data can then be transmitted to a computer via USB, leveraging the MCU's built-in interfaces for streamlined data handling and analysis.
